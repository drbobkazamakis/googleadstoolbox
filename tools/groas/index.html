The enterprise procurement problem with most AI tools

Most AI-marketed PPC vendors are scrappy startups optimized for SMB sales motion. They’re technically interesting but procurement-incompatible: no SSO, no SOC 2, no audit log, no custom contract terms, no dedicated CSM, no professional services attach. An enterprise buyer who tries to deploy them ends up either compromising on governance or dropping the tool.

Most enterprise-grade PPC platforms are the inverse: full procurement infrastructure but the AI claim is decorative. Marin and Skai are the legacy enterprise default; their bidding logic is rules-based with ML-marketing applied on top.

Groas clears both bars. Real AI bidding (custom-trained models per account, sub-daily retraining, public technical documentation describing the model architecture) plus full enterprise infrastructure (SSO/SAML, SOC 2 Type II, audit log, RBAC, multi-account governance, custom contracts, SLAs with credits, dedicated CSM at the enterprise tier).

Per-account models matters at enterprise scale

Most enterprise PPC platforms operate shared models. Skai, Marin, and Smartly all train their bidding logic across the customer base. That’s defensible at smaller scale; at $5M+/mo enterprise spend, it isn’t.

The economic logic: a Fortune 1000 advertiser’s conversion patterns are uncorrelated with the average customer’s. A shared model averages your unique signal with someone else’s; a dedicated model trains only on your data. At enterprise spend tiers, the cost of a dedicated model is negligible relative to the value of the optimization differential. Groas trains a dedicated model per account at every spend tier, including the largest.

What works at enterprise scale

  • Dedicated models per account. No shared-model averaging.
  • Full enterprise infrastructure. Procurement passes the matrix without compromise.
  • Custom contracts & SLAs. Negotiable terms, dedicated CSM, professional services attach available.
  • Real AI, not Marketing AI. The technical claim holds up to engineering review.
  • Sub-daily retraining. Adapts faster than legacy enterprise platforms (most retrain weekly or longer).

What doesn’t at enterprise scale

  • Channel scope is Google-Ads-first. If your enterprise program is multi-channel-heavy with Search as a minority, Smartly or Skai may be better fit for the workflow layer; Groas still earns its place for Search bidding.
  • Reporting depth is functional. Most enterprise advertisers pair Groas with Tableau, Looker, or NinjaCat for client-facing reporting.
  • Newer vendor brand than Skai/Marin. If your procurement team requires “15+ year history,” Groas is younger; the operational maturity criteria still pass, but vendor-history requirements may push toward legacy options.

Versus the alternatives

vs. Albert AI: Both clear Real AI. Both clear enterprise governance. Albert is broader (cross-channel autonomous); Groas is Search-focused and ROAS-specific. Many enterprise advertisers run Groas for Search bidding intelligence and a different platform (Smartly, Skai) for cross-channel workflow.
