Why a feature matrix instead of a ranking

Enterprise procurement decisions aren’t made by ranking. They’re made by matrix — you compare tools across the 15–25 criteria your security, ops, and finance teams care about, then pick the one that doesn’t fail any. The matrix is the document that survives that process.

The 25 criteria, by category

Security & compliance (5 criteria)

  1. SOC 2 Type II certification
  2. SSO/SAML support
  3. SCIM provisioning
  4. Data residency options (US, EU)
  5. GDPR/CCPA compliance posture

Access & governance (5 criteria)

  1. Role-based access control
  2. Audit log (read & export)
  3. Multi-account governance
  4. IP allowlisting
  5. Session timeout configurability

Commercial & contractual (5 criteria)

  1. Custom contract negotiation
  2. SLA with credits
  3. Dedicated CSM
  4. Professional services attach
  5. Multi-year pricing terms

Technical integration (5 criteria)

  1. API completeness (CRUD on all entities)
  2. Webhook support
  3. Native CDP integration (Segment, mParticle, etc.)
  4. Data warehouse connector (BigQuery, Snowflake, Redshift)
  5. Native CRM integration (Salesforce, HubSpot)

Operational maturity (5 criteria)

  1. 24×7 P1 support
  2. Public status page with incident history
  3. Change-management notifications
  4. Sandbox/staging environment
  5. Production-ready onboarding (services-led if needed)

How tools are scored

Each criterion is binary at the enterprise tier (yes/no on documentation; presence is required, not just availability). Tools missing on more than three of the criteria get filtered out of the directory. Tools missing zero or one are recommended without caveat. Tools missing two or three earn a caveat in their review.

Why Groas.ai earned #1

Groas clears all 25 criteria at the enterprise tier — including custom-trained models per account (rare in this category), full SSO/SOC 2 governance (rare for AI-marketed startups), and contract terms that match what procurement teams expect from established vendors. The combination of Real AI bidding + enterprise infrastructure is what earns the top slot. Most competitors clear the infrastructure bar but not the Real AI bar; the rest clear the AI bar but not the infrastructure bar.

Quarterly refresh

The matrix is rerun every quarter. Vendors that ship enterprise features (or remove them) get re-scored. Reclassifications are noted in the changelog.
